# Organizing for Outcomes

This reference condenses the organizational and operating-model lessons from the management notes.

## Structural problem

Most organizations are structured around making things, not achieving outcomes. Teams are grouped by product, platform, page, or channel, while meaningful customer outcomes cut across those boundaries.

## Better operating model

To work by outcomes:

- define user outcome and linked business outcome
- involve design, engineering, and users early
- manage review points by outcome progress
- align intake and prioritization to shared concerns

## Collaboration rule

Move away from design-to-dev handoffs. Use early, lightweight collaboration and user feedback to steer work before large commitments.

## Done rule

Feature work is easy to call done. Outcome work needs:

- hypothesis
- success measure
- review window
- next-step decision rule

Example:

- if outcome moves enough, continue or shift
- if outcome stalls, investigate or pivot

## Backlog reset

When a backlog creates years of false hope, clearing it can be healthier than maintaining the illusion that everything will be delivered.

Use outcome-focused intake:

- what problem do you see?
- what are you worried about?
- what result are you seeking?

This often produces better alignment than asking everyone to rank solutions.

## Team pattern

Organize around major outcome areas plus an operations or run-the-business stream for low-uncertainty work.
