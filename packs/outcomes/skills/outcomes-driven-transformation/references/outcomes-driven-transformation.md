# Outcomes-Driven Transformation

This reference condenses the transformation chapter from the management notes.

## Three rules

1. Colleagues are your customers
2. Everything is an outcome
3. Everything is an experiment

## Colleagues as customers

When a team needs leaders, peers, or internal stakeholders to adopt a strategy, process, or tool, those people should be treated like customers:

- what do they need?
- what problem does this solve for them?
- how will they experience the change?
- what would make them adopt or ignore it?

## Everything is an outcome

For internal transformation, define success as changed behavior, not as a presentation, framework, or rollout plan.

Examples:

- leaders describe strategy the same way
- teams use shared guardrails in checkpoint meetings
- internal partners adopt a new workflow consistently

## Everything is an experiment

Use small tests to learn how people respond:

- pilot a communication pattern
- invite a subset of stakeholders to training
- test a new operating ritual with one team

Then expand only if the behavior change appears.

## Practical template

Use:

- internal customer group
- current behavior
- desired behavior
- intervention to test
- evidence of adoption or change

## PREP-style example

- internal customer: product, ops, and data peers
- desired behavior: they align on metrics before sprint planning
- experiment: lightweight shared planning ritual plus dashboard review
