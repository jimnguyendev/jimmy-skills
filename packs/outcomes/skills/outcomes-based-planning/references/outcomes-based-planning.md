# Outcomes-Based Planning

This reference condenses the roadmap and journey-mapping material.

## Problem with output roadmaps

Feature-and-date roadmaps often present guesses as certainty. Under uncertainty, they become fiction more than plans.

## Better planning model

Use a system of outcomes:

- business impact
- user or customer outcomes
- questions or hypotheses
- experiments or candidate solutions

## Journey map method

Build a map of the current experience:

- who acts
- what they do
- what the system does
- where success or failure happens

Then ask at each step:

- what behaviors predict success?
- what behaviors predict failure?

These become boosters and blockers.

## Roadmap conversion

Convert:

- feature promise -> planning question
- fixed solution -> testable hypothesis
- milestone completion -> outcome review point

## Planning template

Use this structure:

- impact we care about
- behavior change we want
- current blockers or boosters
- belief about what might improve the behavior
- experiment to test the belief
- measure of success

## PREP-style example

- impact: increase learner retention
- outcome: learners complete more daily sessions
- hypothesis: faster feedback improves completion
- test: limited rollout of quicker feedback mechanism
